Subdomain Takeover

Understanding the Threat and How to Prevent It

🛠️ What is Subdomain Takeover?

Subdomain takeover occurs when a subdomain points to an external service (like GitHub, Heroku, etc.) but the service is no longer in use, allowing an attacker to claim it.

🔍 Steps to Identify Subdomain Takeover

  • Find subdomains using tools like Subfinder, Amass, or Sublist3r.
  • Check for dangling DNS records with nslookup or dig.
  • Verify if the service is still active. If not, claim the domain on the service platform.
  • Exploit by hosting malicious content or stealing sensitive information.

🔒 Prevention Methods

  • Regularly audit DNS records to remove unused entries.
  • Use wildcard DNS records cautiously.
  • Enable monitoring and alerts for subdomain changes.
  • Verify ownership before removing external services.

⚙️ Tools for Subdomain Takeover

Here are some popular tools used for detecting subdomain takeover:

  • SubOver – Subdomain takeover scanner.
  • Can I Take Over XYZ – List of services vulnerable to subdomain takeover.
  • Takeover – Another tool for subdomain takeover detection.
